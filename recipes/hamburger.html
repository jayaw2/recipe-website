Ground Beef Burgers

Description

The American cuisine staple: the burger. Several of the dining options on your campus likely offer burgers on their menu. However, if you follow this simple but effective recipe, you will have the skills to create your own less expensive but tastier burger patties with minimal effort. This is a perfect meal to share with friends without having to use a grill.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack of ground beef (preferably close to 80% lean for best taste)
  • Buns of choice (brioche is my personal favorite)
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Garlic Powder
  • Onion Powder
  • Toppings of choice: lettuce, tomato, pickles, onions, cheese, giardinera (personal favorite), etc.

Recipe

  1. Take a handful of ground beef and place on chopping board.
  2. Heavily season beef with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  3. Roll seasoned beef into a large ball with your hands and then flatten with palms. Leave about an inch of thickness for the patty
  4. Indent the middle of one side of patty with the thumb to check for cookedness. Leave about a 2-cm indentation.
  5. Set frying pan on stove on medium-high heat and place two patties on pan. No olive oil needed.
  6. Cook one side of patty for 3-4 minutes on medium-high without touching the patty.
  7. After one side is cooked, flip the patties and do not touch for another 3 minutes. If desired, place a slice of cheese on the cooked side of each patty.
  8. Once both sides are cooked, take off pan and place in plate. If desired toast buns in a toaster and apply topiings, condiments, and sauces of your choice. My personal favorites are cheddar and giardinera on a brioche bun.
